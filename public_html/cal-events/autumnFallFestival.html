Autumn Festival of Art at Holy Spirit Retreat Center

Holy Spirit Retreat Center presents the Autumn Festival of Art, its annual fundraising event, Saturday and Sunday, October 18 and 19, 2014 from 11:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. This family festival offers numerous works of 23 accomplished artists who will be on hand, as well as original arts and craft items for sale, entertainment, food, children's activities and art demonstrations. New this year is a special art walk featuring the artistic works of students from six local high schools.

The Autumn Festival of Art takes place at Holy Spirit Retreat Center's beautiful campus located on 10 acres of hilly, wooded property at 4316 Lanai Road, Encino, CA 91436. The event is presented in the lush, tranquil outdoor areas of the Center as well as indoors in the lovely main building. Attendees can also enjoy a peaceful nature walk around the gardens and lake.

Entertainment includes the Alleluia Dancers, Jon Sherman and his Native American flute, and guitarist Michael O'Palko, all of which can be enjoyed both Saturday and Sunday. The blue grass group Murphy's Flaw will perform on Saturday only.

Professional artists whose works are featured at the Autumn Festival of Art are Marsha Batelaan, Jody Berges, Juli Biondi, Kathi Colman, Terese Denham, CSJ, David Deyell and Pleine Aire Painters, Sandy Diamond, Michelle Domecus, Denny Howard, Cherryl Green, Dalton Gudleman, Cal and Mary Hubbard, Tommy Hollenstein, Denny Howard, Rob Jacobs, Sr. Thien Anh Khong, JMA, Susanna Kuan, Nancy (Mei Jong) Liaw, Rafael Maniago, Darlene Mellein, Susan Merritt, Manzal Rassouli, Boi Sibug, Nancy Snooks and Magoo Valencia-Carson.

Participating art students represent the following area high schools: Bellarmine Jefferson, Bishop Alemany, Crespi Carmelite, Flintridge Sacred Heart Academy, La Reina, Louisville, Notre Dame and Providence.

Admission tickets are $10.00 per person, $25 per family and can be purchased at the Festival. Children 12 years and under are free. All proceeds go to support the ministry of Holy Spirit Retreat Center which is a 501 ( c ) ( 3 ) non-profit organization.

Holy Spirit Retreat Center, as a chartered ministry of the Sisters of Social Service, is rooted in Scripture, and the vision and spirit of Saint Benedict. The Center strives to create an atmosphere of prayer and hospitality where community is nourished, each person can thrive, and an interfaith environment is fostered. The Center is open 7 days a week and presents an array of retreats, conferences, workshops, and seminars throughout the year.
